Albert Einstein

"Once we accept our limits, we go beyond them."

Even after producing the special relativity equation E = mc2, Einstein's work still remained controversial. He was, however, accepted by leading physicists.

Notable Events:

  • Born March 14, 1879
  • At age 5 his father, an engineer showed him a compass. Albert was fascinated that some force was directing the needle.
  • He finishes high school at 17 and enrolls in the Zürich Polytechnic, renouncing German citizenship.
  • In 1900 he graduates from the Zürich Polytechnic with a degree in physics.
  • Einstein's General Theory of Relativity is completed as he has been appointed processor of theoretical physics at the Federal Institute of Technology in Zürich.
  • A year later his General Theory of Relativity is published.
  • In 1921 he is awarded the Nobel Prize in physics.
  • As now Adolf Hitler is Chancellor of Germany, Einstein in 1933 emigrated to the United States to land a position at New Jersey's Institute for Advanced Study.
  • 1939 sees the beginning of WWII. Einstein warns Roosevelt that Germany is likely working on building an atomic bomb.
  • Becomes a citizen of the United States in 1940.
  • Retains a position with the Research and Development Division of the U.S. Navy on Ammunition and Explosives.
  • Works in favor of disarmament.
  • Dies April 17, 1955 at age 76.

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. For knowledge is limited, whereas imagination embraces the entire world, stimulating progress, giving birth to evolution. It is, strictly speaking, a real factor in scientific research."

Albert Einstein
